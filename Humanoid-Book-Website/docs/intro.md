# Introduction to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## The Dawn of Physical AI

We stand at the threshold of a new era in artificial intelligence - the age of Physical AI. While traditional AI operates in the digital realm, Physical AI bridges the gap between artificial intelligence and the physical world through sophisticated robotic systems. This textbook focuses on the pinnacle of this integration: humanoid robots that can perceive, reason, and act in human environments.

Physical AI represents the convergence of large language models, computer vision, robotics, and human-centered design to create systems that can understand and interact with the physical world in ways that were previously thought to be exclusively human capabilities.

## What Are Humanoid Robots?

Humanoid robots are robots with physical forms that approximate the human body structure, typically featuring:
- A head with sensory systems (cameras, microphones, etc.)
- A torso
- Two arms with articulated hands
- Two legs with feet for locomotion

But the form is just the beginning. The true value of humanoid robots lies in their ability to operate in human-centered environments designed for people - from door handles to stairs, from kitchens to office spaces.

## Why Humanoid Robotics Matters Now

The field of humanoid robotics is experiencing unprecedented growth driven by:

1. **Advances in AI**: Modern LLMs, computer vision, and planning algorithms
2. **Improved Hardware**: Better actuators, sensors, and computing platforms
3. **Real-World Applications**: Service industries, healthcare, education, and manufacturing
4. **Integration Capabilities**: Better integration with existing human environments

## The AI-Native Approach

This textbook takes an AI-native approach to humanoid robotics, considering how AI systems can be designed from the ground up to work with physical robotic systems. Rather than adding AI as an afterthought to existing robotic architectures, we explore how to create systems where AI and physical embodiment are designed together.

## Prerequisites and Technical Requirements

This textbook is designed to be accessible to a wide audience, but you'll get the most value if you have:

- Basic programming experience in Python
- Understanding of linear algebra and calculus
- Familiarity with Linux command line
- Access to a computer capable of running robot simulations (8GB+ RAM, multi-core processor)

For the technical implementation:
- ROS 2 Humble Hawksbill (or later)
- Ubuntu 22.04 or Windows with WSL2
- Python 3.8+
- Gazebo simulation environment
